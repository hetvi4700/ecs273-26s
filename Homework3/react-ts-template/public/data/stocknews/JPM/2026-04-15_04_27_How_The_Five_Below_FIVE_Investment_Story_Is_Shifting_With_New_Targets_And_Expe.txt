The latest analyst update on Five Below lifts the fair value estimate from US$229.32 to US$261.32, implying a higher long term price target for the shares. That shift lines up with recent Street commentary, where bullish voices are moving targets into the mid to high US$200s, while more cautious analysts still question how much optimism is already priced in. BofA, JPMorgan and KGI Securities are among the firms setting targets around the mid US$200s, framing Five Below as a higher multiple story tied to improving results under new leadership. Several banks including Morgan Stanley, Barclays, UBS and Citi have lifted price targets. This signals confidence in Five Below’s store economics and growth runway being reflected in higher implied valuations. Recent initiations and upgrades, such as KGI Securities starting at Outperform and William Blair’s upgrade, show new bullish voices joining existing coverage rather than coverage being pulled back. Barclays, which keeps an Equal Weight rating alongside a higher target, highlights that expectations across dollar and value retail are already high. Execution therefore needs to match what is implied in current pricing. Mizuho, maintaining a Neutral stance while raising its target ahead of the Q4 report, explicitly flags that the setup comes with high expectations. Five Below updated its first quarter fiscal 2026 outlook, guiding to net sales of US$1.18b to US$1.20b, supported by plans for about 45 net new stores and an assumed 14% to 16% increase in comparable sales. The company expects first quarter fiscal 2026 net income of US$86 million to US$93 million and diluted EPS of US$1.55 to US$1.67 on roughly 55.6 million diluted shares. For the full fiscal year 2026, Five Below guided to net sales of US$5.20b to US$5.30b, with plans to open about 150 net new stores and an assumed 3% to 5% increase in comparable sales. Five Below reported no additional share repurchases between November 2, 2025 and January 31, 2026. Total buybacks under its current authorization stand at 449,324 shares for US$69.46 million, or about 0.82% of shares. Fair value raised from US$229.32 to US$261.32, an increase of about 14% in the implied long term estimate. Revenue growth assumption adjusted from 11.77% to 10.59% for future dollar revenue expansion. Net profit margin assumption moved from 7.46% to 8.42% for expected profitability on future dollar sales. Future P/E multiple revised from 35.0x to 34.0x on projected earnings. Discount rate adjusted from 8.54% to 8.64% as the required return in the model.
