Several firms, including Evercore ISI, Needham, Barclays, Citi, Truist, Roth Capital and others, raised Alphabet price targets in early February, reflecting supportive views around AI related opportunities, Q4 results and the company’s ability to monetize its products. Wells Fargo upgraded Alphabet to Overweight and raised its target, highlighting strengths in customer data, distribution and compute capacity, and pointing to Project Google as a key factor in its long term AI infrastructure build out. Tigress Financial lifted its target on Alphabet, pointing to exposure to multiple growth engines tied to AI centric trends, cash generation and shareholder value creation as key parts of the longer term story. Citi most recently raised its Alphabet target again and placed the stock on an upside 90 day catalyst watch, signaling that the firm sees identifiable company specific drivers to track over the near term. Stifel’s reduction of its Alphabet price target in April, along with legal commentary from TD Cowen and Bernstein around Meta and Google related verdicts, underlines concerns about ongoing regulatory and legal overhangs that could affect investor conviction. Alphabet is raising about US$15b to US$20b through a multi tranche U.S. dollar bond sale, alongside a separate £997.32m offering, as it taps fixed income markets with a broad syndicate of co lead underwriters. A Los Angeles jury found Google and Meta liable in a social media addiction case, and research notes are highlighting legal risk and the potential for ongoing litigation pressure on large platforms. Alphabet's self driving unit Waymo is valued at US$126b in a recent fundraising round, with reports that it plans to raise US$16b in financing while U.S. regulators scale back some robotaxi expansions. Google is expanding its AI and cloud footprint through a multibillion dollar TPU rental deal with Meta, a CVS Health partnership using Gemini models and healthcare APIs, and talks to invest US$100m in cloud startup Fluidstack, while regulators in the EU, U.S. and Australia increase scrutiny of its search and AI services.
